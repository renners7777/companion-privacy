🛡️ Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 5, 2026

Our Privacy Promise: Stroke Survivor Companion is structured under an offline-first architecture. absolutely zero personal health metrics, symptoms, clinical notes, or routines are aggregated, transmitted, or hosted on remote cloud servers.

This Privacy Policy describes how Stroke Survivor Companion ("we", "our", or "the App") collects, uses, protects, and governs personal health telemetry, task tracking checklist details, custom symptom diaries, caregiver messages logs, and general user configuration info.

1. Offline-First Privacy Guarantee

All symptom logs, medication or rehab task routines checklists, journal entries, secure conversations history, and personalized notifications profiles are exclusively generated and processed on your client device. Data is compiled locally within sandboxed file structures in a secure Room SQLite database. We don’t maintain external storage endpoints, central server systems, databases, or analytics portals capable of examining your on-device activity. Your logs remain private, secure, and under your absolute control.

2. Cryptographic Partner Synchronization

To enable coordinated logging and synchronization with a selected home partner or clinical caregiver:

  • Users must actively consent and share an individual invite code string.
  • Peer-to-peer exchanges and status tracking sync actions execute secure cryptographic handshake processes using a localized sync mechanism.
  • These linked sync connections are encapsulated using secure pairing mechanisms. No centralized storage structures hold the cleartext messages logs or patient metrics history. Only matched companion keys can decode the sync transmissions.

3. Compliance and Device Regulations

The App enforces Google Play Developer Console and User Data guidelines:

  • No Third-Party Analytics: We do not code, integrate, or reference telemetry SDKs, ad-trackers, social logins, or commercial performance profiles.
  • No Native Remote Databases: Unlike conventional health portals, there is no remote cloud sync. Medical privacy remains absolute on your local hardware.

4. Sovereignty and Deletion Rights (GDPR & CCPA)

Since health indicators are kept solely under on-device configurations, you carry complete self-determination and permanent right-to-be-forgotten coverage:

  • You can easily initiate a purge by clicking the "Purge Offline Storage Cache" command in the app Settings screen.
  • This action immediately, permanently, and irreversibly deletes the local SQLite database container, erasing all symptom logs, companion keys, checklist states, and notes instantly.

5. Updates to This Policy

This local data disclosure might receive minor iterations as Android hardware standards modify. We recommend reviewing the integrated privacy terms embedded right inside the app settings to confirm active compliance patterns.
